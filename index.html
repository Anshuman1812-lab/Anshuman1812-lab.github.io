About

I am Anshuman Singh, a passionate and results-driven cybersecurity professional with a deep commitment to protecting digital landscapes and fostering secure environments. With a strong foundation in computer science and advanced specialization in cybersecurity, I thrive on leveraging cutting-edge technologies and best practices to safeguard systems and data from emerging threats.

My expertise lies in assessing and mitigating risks, leveraging cutting-edge tools and methodologies to secure IT environments. I have worked with diverse organizations to defend against cyber threats and ensure compliance with industry standards and regulations. By the way, check out my work.

I hold a Master's degree in Cybersecurity & Privacy from New Jersey Institute of Technology, where I researched innovative approaches to GDPR compliance and healthcare data protection. My Bachelor's in Computer Science from Kurukshetra University laid the groundwork for my technical acumen and analytical skills.

Outside of work, I contribute to the cybersecurity community through writing thought pieces on Medium about cybersecurity trends and wargames, and engaging in ethical hacking competitions to keep sharpening my skills. Apart from this, I also enjoy contributing to open-source projects, and exploring innovative ways to bridge AI and security.

Experience

Throughout my dynamic career in cybersecurity, I have woven a tapestry of impactful experiences that demonstrate my dedication to protecting digital environments and ensuring data integrity.

My journey began at the National Institute of Technical Teachers Training and Research (NITTTR) in Chandigarh, where as an intern, I delved into the realm of network security. There, I implemented encryption protocols and blockchain technologies to fortify healthcare networks. This formative experience laid a strong foundation in cryptography and risk mitigation, equipping me with the skills needed to counter emerging threats effectively.

Building on this solid base, I transitioned to a Cyber Security Analyst role at Infosys. In this position, I conducted vulnerability assessments and developed ITIL-compliant security measures that bolstered the organization's defenses. My efforts in cybersecurity training and incident response significantly enhanced the company's resilience against threats, while my commitment to 24/7 Tier 1 support ensured a remarkable 99% infrastructure uptime, driving operational excellence.

My expertise further expanded at NJIT's Ying Wu College of Computing, where I served as a Data Protection & Privacy Researcher. Here, I led initiatives to enhance Android application security and GDPR compliance. Through meticulous analysis of over 10,000 applications, I identified critical vulnerabilities in healthcare systems and pioneered AI-powered threat detection methodologies. This role highlighted my ability to improve privacy safeguards and establish scalable, effective solutions for mobile security.

Currently, as a Cyber Security Specialist at Rebecca Everlene Trust Company, I have developed and implemented a comprehensive cybersecurity strategy for the College ASPECTS platform. By introducing advanced security measures such as Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) and Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), I have fortified system security and ensured compliance with GDPR and COPPA regulations. My work has not only safeguarded sensitive data but also aligned robust security practices with the organization's strategic objectives.

This journey through various facets of cybersecurity has equipped me with a diverse skill set and a wealth of experience, enabling me to tackle complex security challenges and contribute to the creation of secure digital environments.

Projects

GDPR Android Threat Analysis is a comprehensive environment designed for evaluating and executing privacy threat detection tools in the context of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). This testbed enables users to analyze various privacy threats associated with mobile applications, particularly Android apps, and ensure compliance with GDPR standards.

The OSINT Gathering project is designed to collect and analyze publicly available information about a target (e.g., domain, IP address) using various open-source tools and libraries. This project demonstrates techniques for reconnaissance and surveillance using tools like Recon-ng, theHarvester, and Shodan, and methods for web scraping and browser automation.

This project implements an automated system for network threat detection and analysis using Python scripts, network monitoring tools, and AI-powered report generation. The system integrates Wireshark, TCPdump, Scapy, Nmap, and OpenAI's GPT-3 API to monitor network traffic, perform ethical hacking techniques, and generate detailed threat analysis reports automatically.

This virtual job simulation involved conducting a thorough analysis of potential cybersecurity threats along with the AIG's Cyber Defense Unit, based on CISA guidelines. I used tools like Wireshark and TCPdump to monitor network traffic and identify possible threats. Additionally, I created custom Python scripts to help with ethical hacking techniques and used other Python tools for deeper threat analysis, making the overall threat detection process more effective.

Back in my learning days, I simulated various network threat scenarios using Cisco Packet Tracer to understand and improve network security. This included assessing vulnerabilities to attacks like DDoS and Man-in-the-Middle (MitM). I also incorporated real-time threat detection techniques and implemented measures such as access control lists and secure communication methods to enhance the network's security and resilience.
